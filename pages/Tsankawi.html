Tsankawi Ruins is one of the more scenic trails with some very interesting features beyond the unexcavated ruins themselves. Situated on a low mesa overlooking the Sangre de Cristo mountains and the Rio Grande valley, the paths to the top are so well worn that the stone itself is grooved deeply. There are ladder access points: the main entrance one is not high and leads one to a ledge that circumnavigates the mesa; the second is much higher and is optional - it returns to the same ledge as the first, and along its path petroglyphs can be found. Beautiful views abound, and walking along the mesa's top, where the ruins are still buried, one can find numerous pottery fragments. These should remain as they are for others to see. Views from the mesa top are spectacular and make for an unforgettable experience. While this trail is part of the Bandelier National Monument, it is detached from the main body, situated on Highway 4 near White Rock. Both the National Park Service and the American Southwest have websites, the latter being a bit more informative. There are also some nice blogs on Tsankawi.
