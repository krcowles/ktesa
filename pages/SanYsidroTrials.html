TRAIL TIPS!

FIRST: The San Ysidro Trials area is used locally for dirt bike trials several times a year, so please check online (NMTA Home Page) to make sure you are not going when one of those events is scheduled.

SECOND: It is best to drive up to the parking lot (1.2 miles from US 550), but this requires a key to pass the locked gate. A key can be obtained on a first-come, first-served basis from the BLM office, 100 Sun Ave NE, Albuquerque. The key can be retained for several days, if needed. Please Note (!): the dirt road is rather tricky and requires 4-wheel drive with good clearance.

Although the San Ysidro Trials area is used for several events, most of the year it is vacated. Drive up to the parking area (see Trail Tips) where there is an information kiosk. Park anywhere, but the trail begins behind the kiosk (north) and quickly turns east. There are no trail markers! There are occasional stone cairns. More than one possibility exists for hiking, depending on which way you proceed when you encounter a fork about .2 mile in: one going north, one continuing east. There are two canyons in the area: the East Canyon and the West Canyon. This hike illustrates the latter.
