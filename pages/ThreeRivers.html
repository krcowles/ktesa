The Three Rivers Petroglyphs site is the most prolific petroglyph site in New Mexico, but requires significant travel from the Albuquerque area to reach. Located in the Tularosa basin, it is about a three hour drive, and located off US 54. To have more time to see the over 21,000 glyphs, it would be advisable to stay overnight in either Carrizozo or Tularosa. The hike is not long, and requires easy ascent of approximately 150 ft from the parking area, but affords views of a significant number of inscriptions. The style of the petroglyphs is markedly different from the style of the Rio Grande, found in the Albuquerque area. More information can be found on the BLM Site.
