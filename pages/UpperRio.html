TRAIL TIPS!

As the Rio En Medio trail descends along the beautiful cascading creek, there is a point of departure towards Aspen Ranch which is a bit tricky. The trail actually cuts across the creek at a not-so-obvious point (see red flag on geomap above). The trail otherwise appears to go merrily along in a straight fashion, but ends up at a boundary fence with no place to go but up a very steep hill. While the authors did this very thing, it is not recommended!

The Upper Rio En Medio Trail is downhill to its point of departure towards Aspen Ranch. The entire path downhill is a beautiful series of cascades and the trail spends a good amount of time in the shade of tall firs and aspens. At one point early on, the trail cuts through a lovely forest meadow. The terminus is Aspen Ranch, for which the trail ascends slightly. Most on-line information addresses the hike from the other end to approach the falls, which is not a part of this hike. That section is referred to in the book (below) as "Lower Rio En Medio"
