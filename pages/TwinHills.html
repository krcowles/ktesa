TRAIL TIPS!

Forest Road 24 begins at a cattle guard where NM62 ends. There are jeep tracks heading west from the marker identified in the driving directions. With 4-wheel drive, at least part of this road is navigable and can save significant hiking over the mesa to get to Twin Hills. The authors proceeded about 1.2 miles in before parking and proceeding on foot. The trail in the map starts at that point, rather than at the aforementioned marker.

Twin Hills is a volcanic geological feature identified in the Montoso Quad map of the USGS near Santa Fe. The hike begins by following the jeep trail, but near the Twin Hills, the hike is not so discernible. As the area is filled with other volcanic "spires" and "cones", the opportunities to explore are many. This hike proceeded south from the north hill to some nearby volcanic spires. The way back, to form a loop, was guided by the book reference below, which identified a hogback ridge en route to the original jeep track. The views from the volcanic peaks are well worth the trip.
