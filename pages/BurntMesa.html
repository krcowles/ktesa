Burnt Mesa, a finger of land, (part of the Pajarito Plateau which overlooks Frijoles Canyon and some side canyons) is part of the Bandelier National Monument territory. It acquired its name as a result of fires in 1977, but no longer retains the obvious scars from that event. Nonetheless, most of the mesa is an open area filled with grasses and all kinds of flowers and bushes, as well as occasional groups of medium-sized Ponderosas. It turns out that autumn is an excellent time for that hike, as the fall flowers are practically glowing a mixture of colors: yellows, reds, purples, whites. The hike actually done was a combination of the hike outlined in Reference 1, below, and an adventure off-trail leading through scrub brush in an attempt to locate a trail by this name found on an app. That trail was never located, but the authors did find their way back! The suggested hike is the one outlined in the book.
