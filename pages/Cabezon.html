Cabezon Peak is a volcanic "neck" - the remnants of a volcano whose exterior has eroded away. It is located in a Wilderness Study Area and is maintained by the BLM. Access is secured by travelling west from US 550 towards San Luis, and then on dirt roads to the trailhead, which is clearly marked. The road to the parking area is quite rough and requires a vehicle with sufficient ground clearance. From the parking area, the trail immediately ascends the steep slope towards the peak itself. From the top of the slope, and en route, there are numerous fine views. For more historical and general information about Cabezon, visit the BLM Site. Allegedly, one can reach the very top of the peak, however the author discovered that it is a treacherous slope of loose rock and requires significant skill. Ropes or other climbing accessories would be helpful.
