This badlands area, just west of Cuba, NM, is one of five areas (see map above) that are designated wilderness areas accessible to the public. While there are no specific trails, each of the five has unique characteristics, ranging from variegated hills, to hoodoos, to petrified wood. The Mesa de Cuba is the first area encountered of the five when traveling from US550. Parking is basically wherever you wish to begin your adventure. In the case, the bend in the dirt road where it heads west. Don't attempt this road if weather conditions are not good - the authors almost got stuck in the mud in March when snow melt was still present, and had to turn around and leave. The hiking area was introduced to the authors on more than one occasion by the New Mexico Magazine: March 2003, January 2007, and November 2013.

The mesas form a part of the San Juan Basin Badlands
