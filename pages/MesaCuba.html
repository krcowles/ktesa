This badlands area, just west of Cuba, NM, is one of five areas (see map above) that are designated wilderness areas accessible to the public. While there are no specific trails, each of the five has unique characteristics, ranging from variegated hills, to hoodoos, to petrified wood. The Mesa de Cuba is the first area encountered of the five when traveling from US550. The hiking area was introduced to the authors on more than one occasion by the New Mexico Magazine: March 2003, January 2007, and November 2013. The mesas form a part of the San Juan Basin Badlands, and references can be found online. To see the large number of badlands in the basin, checkout the SJB Badlands Map. Most of the area is exposed to sun, so be prepared for the desert-like conditions. The roads can also be virtually impassable in wet or snowy weather, as they are dirt/gravel and traverse low areas.
