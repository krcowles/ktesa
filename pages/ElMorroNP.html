El Morro National Monument Index of Hikes

Directions to the El Morro Visitor Center

El Morro National Monument is known for its inscriptions of those who passed before, as it was located on an ancient east-west trail. It also offered a precious commodity - water - and was hence a necessary spot to visit when traveling through the area. The rock inscriptions range from those created by ancient Indians to those added by the more modern Spanish Conquistadors. One of the more famous Spaniards is Don Juan Onate, the first governor of the territory. Later on, a survey team from the Union Pacific Railroad (UPR) passed through and left its mark.

Owing to the convenient source of water, an ancient pueblo was built on top of the towering rock overlooking the pool, and its ruins are now part of the attraction. It is unclear how long the ruins were in use, and archeologists believe that Indians were living in the area prior to building the pueblo. Like many of the southwest ancient ruins, the people left somewhere around 1200-1300BC.
