Both the Red Dot and the Blue Dot Trails have their trailheads in White Rock, NM, on the rim of Pajarito Canyon. At the bottom, their is a connecting trail called the River Trail, which parallels the Rio Grande, and joins the Red Dot to the Blue Dot. The Red Dot seems to be a bit steeper than the Blue Dot, and when making the loop, the Red Dot seems a better starting point. The loop can be joined on the rim with another interconnecting trail, or if there are two cars available, park one at each trailhead for a shorter overall hike. The Red Dot to River Trail to Blue Dot loop through the canyon is about 4.4 miles in length [web ref: Los Alamos Trails; hiking books: Day Hikes in the Santa Fe Area, by the Sierra Club; Hiking New Mexico, by Laurence Parent ]. One of the pleasant features of this hike is the appearance of water features along Pajarito Spring: quiet pools, small waterfalls, etc. - depending on time of year.
