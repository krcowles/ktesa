El Morro National Monument is a place of both beauty and of history. It has been known by native people as a place to find water in an otherwise arid land; it was a place the Spanish Conquistadors passed through and left their mark; it was a place where the US Geological Survey also passed - those, and many more inscriptions still can be found on El Morro's walls. Located west of El Malpais on Highway 53, it is a striking monument of sandstone. Loop trails guide the visitor past both inscriptions and an ancient ruin on top of the bluff - a hike well worth taking. The views along the way and on top are quite lovely.
