TRAIL TIPS!

The trail is described as a "balloon" (aka "lollipop") trail that proceeds out from the trailhead a distance, and then makes a short loop. The return portion of the loop traverses the Bike & Hike trail, is paved and is somewhat busy. A better option is to make the trip an "out-and-back" hike to the endpoint near the Montano Road bridge, and avoid the popular paved trail.

The Rio Grande Nature Center (RGNC) is home not only to informative nature walks, events, gift shop, and bird viewing room (with floor-to-ceiling glass), but also to several short hikes in the Rio Grande Bosque. This particular hike is called the "Aldo Leopold Trail", dedicated to the efforts of the wildlife conservationist. While the trail is easy and flat, the views of the "Aldo Leopold Forest" (part of the Bosque), and the Rio Grande are lovely. The walk is peaceful and perhaps less frequently traveled than the other trails at the Nature Center owing to its longer length.
