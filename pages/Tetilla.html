Tetilla Peak is a somewhat desolate area southwest of Santa Fe, acessed on a rutted dirt/gravel road labeled FR 24. There is no trail to ascend the peak, but the book Day Hikes In The Santa Fe Area by the Northern New Mexico Group of the Sierra Club does have a reasonable description of travel to and up the peak. En route, nearer the top, there are stone cairns marking the route previous hikers have taken. Basically, reasonable instincts are required to ascertain the best way to the top. From the top, there are great views of the surrounding area in every direction. In trying to find websites describing the hike, what was mostly encountered were descriptions of the nearby Tetilla Peak Recreation Area within the Cochiti Pueblo reservation. Tetilla Peak is apparently managed by the Santa Fe National Forest Service.
