The Domingo Baca Trail, though not officially maintained, is nonetheless relatively easy to follow - though steep in parts, and requiring a bit of scrambling. Due to its elevation, the hike is demanding. The trail leads directly under the Sandia Tramway, from where one can spot bits of the wreckage. Once deep in the canyon, the view is limited. The wreckage is actually spread out over a wide area, but a plaque has been placed near the first piece encountered, which provides a bit of detail about the crash. The Sandia Mountain Hiking Guide by Mike Coltrin, contains good information about the hike, including travel and GPS waypoints to keep you on track. The same basic information can be accessed online at Sandia Hiking.com.
