The Bandera Ice Cave is located just off of Highway 53, about 24 miles south of the I-40 exit, and is not part of the National Monument - it is privately owned, and a fee is paid to visit the cave. The trail is only about a mile or so, and proceeds up part of the Bandera Lava Flow and then to the ice cave, where legend has it that it was used for many years by locals to cool and preserve food stuffs. Access to the cave is provided by a staircase which descends and cools as one proceeds. The Bandera Crater is the largest crater of the El Malpais area, and is one of the youngest - worth a visit.
