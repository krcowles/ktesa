TRAIL TIPS!

Since the trail (#231) is not officially maintained, it can be tricky to follow. It is highly advisable to use the waypoints provided by the book referenced below. Important trail notes are provided that correspond with the waypoints. Once near the top, you will find rock cairns that help keep you on track.

The hike is rated "Difficult", and it truly is -- not owing to the length, or even just the elevation change, but due to the relentless steepness of the trail its entire duration. Also, there are not many vistas along the way, although there are some nice glimpses of Agua Sarca Canyon. Coming back down the trail can be a constant stress on the lower legs without numerous breaks. The trail is sometimes a bit overgrown with vegetation, especially near the summit. Nonetheless, the view at the top, where it joins the North Crest Trail, is lovely.
