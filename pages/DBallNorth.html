The Dale Ball Trails on the north side of Santa Fe represent an extensive network of interconnected trails, allowing the hiker to create a hike of almost any length. The trails also connect with some other area hikes, like the Dorothy Stewart Trail, the Nature Conservancy Trail, etc. While there are numerous ups and downs, usually 100-300 ft, there seems to be no sustained elevation change. Great views of the Santa Fe area are afforded. The Dale Ball Trails are mentioned in the book Day Hikes In The Santa Fe Area, Northern New Mexico Group of the Sierra Club, there are also on-line resources. These include the Santa Fe Conservation Trust as well as links to maps and other relevant data. The online album is located here.
