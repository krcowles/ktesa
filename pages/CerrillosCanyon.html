The Cerrillos Hills State Park abuts BLM land to its west, thereby forming a hiking opportunity that is not in the park proper. The book reference listed below shows it as an "Exploratory Route", and actually there are some other trails that can be explored branching off from the hike shown on the map. Regardless, the trail ascends slowly up an arroyo and into the canyon areas of Cerrillos Hills. A spur trail, north of the loop, provides access to an old mine shaft and Grand Central Mountain, which allegedly provides some great area views, including the escarpment of La Bajada. The scenery is quite different than that of the State Park, so the hike makes for a rather different excursion. The trailhead access is an unmarked dirt road, just off of CR57 (the dirt road can be seen using the Satellite View on google maps).
