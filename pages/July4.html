The Fourth of July Trail begins at the campground of the same name in the Monzano Mountains, near Tajique, off of NM 55. The area is well known for its Bigtooth Maples, which provide beautiful reds and oranges in the autumn - a contrast to the mostly yellow of the aspens and cottonwoods so prevalent in the mountains. The trail ascends and then veers west to join the Crest Trail, and when followed north takes the hiker to a nice view of the valley and nearby peaks. Because the name of the trail is the same as some other trails, notably in Colorado, make sure to zone in on the Monzano Mountain hike sites. One site with numerous waypoints given is the Trimble Outdoors site. The books Hiking New Mexico by laurence Parent, and 100 Hikes In New Mexico by Craig Martin both supply good trail and travel information.
